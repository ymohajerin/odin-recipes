Pizza Napoletana Recipe

Pizza Napoletana, also known as Neapolitan Pizza, is an iconic culinary delight that hails from Naples, Italy. Celebrated for its simplicity, fresh flavors, and traditional cooking method, this pizza is a world-renowned representation of Italian gastronomic heritage.
The Pizza Napoletana is characterized by its soft, thin, and slightly charred crust, a result of baking in extremely high temperatures in a wood-fired oven. Despite its thinness, the dough is also notably chewy, showcasing the perfect balance of texture that's neither too crisp nor too soft.
The toppings adhere to the principle of minimalism and quality over quantity. A traditional Pizza Napoletana is typically topped with San Marzano tomatoes, grown in the volcanic soil around Mount Vesuvius, giving them a sweet flavor and low acidity. This is complemented with fresh, creamy buffalo mozzarella, a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, a scattering of fresh basil leaves, and a pinch of sea salt.
Every bite of Pizza Napoletana offers a harmonious blend of flavors - the sweet tanginess of the tomatoes, the creamy richness of the mozzarella, the aromatic basil, and the smoky hint from the wood-fired cooking process. It's not just a pizza; it's an experience that transports you to the vibrant streets of Naples.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of 00 Italian flour
  • 1 1/2 cups of warm water
  • 2 teaspoons of sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon active dry yeast
  • 1 can (14 ounces) of San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • Sea salt to taste
  • 8 ounces of fresh buffalo mozzarella, sliced or torn into chunks
  • A handful of fresh basil leaves

instructions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine the warm water and yeast, stirring until the yeast is dissolved. Let it sit for a few minutes to activate.
  3. Gradually add the water and yeast mixture to the flour, mixing until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 10 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 2 hours, or until it doubles in size.
  6. In a bowl, combine the crushed San Marzano tomatoes, extra virgin olive oil, and salt. Set aside.
  7. Slice or tear the mozzarella into chunks, and set aside.
  8. Preheat your wood-fired oven to a temperature of around 900 degrees Fahrenheit (480 degrees Celsius). If you are using a home oven, preheat it to its maximum temperature, usually around 500 degrees Fahrenheit (260 degrees Celsius), and place a pizza stone inside if you have one.
  9. Divide the dough into two portions. On a floured surface, shape one portion into a thin round base. Make sure to leave the edges slightly thicker to form the crust.
  10. Spread half of the tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border for the crust.
  11. Arrange half of the mozzarella pieces on top of the sauce.
  12. Carefully transfer the pizza to the oven (use a pizza peel if available) and bake for about 1-2 minutes in a wood-fired oven or about 10-12 minutes in a home oven, or until the crust is golden and slightly charred, and the cheese is bubbling and slightly golden.
  13. Remove the pizza from the oven, scatter a few fresh basil leaves on top, and drizzle with a little more olive oil if desired.
  14. Serve the pizza hot, cut into slices.
